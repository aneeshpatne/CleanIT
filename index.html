3 R Principle

Throughout most of history, the amount of waste generated by humans was insignificant due to low levels of population density and exploitation of natural resources. Common waste produced during pre-modern times was mainly ashes and human biodegradable waste, and these were released back into the ground locally, with minimum environmental impact. Tools made out of wood or metal were generally reused or passed down through the generations. Following the onset of industrialisation and the sustained urban growth of large population centres in India, the buildup of waste in the cities caused a rapid deterioration in levels of sanitation and the general quality of urban life
